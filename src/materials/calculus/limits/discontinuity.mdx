# Identifying Discontinuity

There are three types of discontinuity:

## Jump Discontinuity

![Jump Discontinuity](/materials/jump_discontinuity.png "Jump Discontinuity")

This happens when $\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow a^-} f(x) \neq \lim_{x\rightarrow a^+} f(x)$

---

## Infinite Discontinuity

![Infinite Discontinuity](/materials/infinite_discontinuity.png "Infinite Discontinuity")

This happens when the function approaches infinity or negative infinity, whether from the left side or the right side.

---

## Removable Discontinuity

![Removable Discontinuity](/materials/removable_discontinuity.png "Removable Discontinuity")

This happens when $\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow a} f(x) = f(a)$, or $f(a)$ does not exist.

# Identifying Discontinuity without Graph: Example

<Example>
The functions $f$, $g$, and $h$ are defined below, along with some $x$ values. Determine the discontinuities each of this function has at its respective given point.

$$
f(x)=\frac{1}{x}\text{, at }x=0
$$
<br />
$$
g(x)=\begin{cases}
x^2& \text{, } x \leq2\\
x& \text{, } x > 2\\
\end{cases}\text{, at }x=2
$$
<br />
$$
h(x)=\begin{cases}
\cos{x} \text{, } x \neq 0\\
x& \text{, } x = 0\\
\end{cases}\text{, at }x=2
$$

</Example>

Since $\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow 0^-} f(x) = -\infty$ and $\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow 0^+} f(x) = \infty$, $f$ has infinite discontinuity at $x=0$.

Since $\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow 0^-} g(x) = 4$ and $\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow 0^+} g(x) = 2$, $g$ has jump discontinuity at $x=2$.

Since $\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow 0^-} h(x) = 1$, $\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow 0^+} h(x) = 1$, and they are equal, $h$ has removable discontinuity at $x=1$.
