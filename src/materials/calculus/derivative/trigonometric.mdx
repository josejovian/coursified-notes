# Derivatives of Trigonometric Functions

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
1.\quad&f(x)=\sin{x}\\
&f'(x)=\cos{x}\\
2.\quad&f(x)=\cos{x}\\
&f'(x)=-\sin{x}\\
3.\quad&f(x)=\tan{x}\\
&f'(x)=\sec^2{x}\\
4.\quad&f(x)=\csc{x}\\
&f'(x)=-\csc{x}\cot{x}\\
5.\quad&f(x)=\sec{x}\\
&f'(x)=\sec{x}\tan{x}\\
6.\quad&f(x)=\cot{x}\\
&f'(x)=\csc^2{x}\\
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Memorizing the above basic derivation is useful, as well as remembering basic trigonometric identities. By using the first two derivation (sin and cos), you can obtain the derivation of the remaining trigonometric functions.

# Derivatives of Trigonometric Functions: Examples

<Example>
If $f(x) = 4 \sin^2{2x} - 3 \tan{x}$, calculate $f'(x)$.
</Example>

Remember the chain rule, and the product rule.

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= 4 \sin^2{2x} - 3 \tan{x}\\
&= 4 (\sin{2x} \sin{2x}) - 3 \tan{x}\\
f'(x) &= 4 (2 \cos{2x} \sin{2x} + 2 \sin{2x} \cos{2x}) - 3 \sec^2{x}\\
&= 16 \sin{2x} \cos{2x} - 3 \sec^2{x}\\
\end{align*}
$$

---

<Example>
Show that $\frac{d}{dx}\tan{x} = \sec^2{x}$.
</Example>

Recall the quotient rule, and the trigonometric identity $\sin^2{x}+\cos^2{x} = 1$.

$$
\begin{align*}
y &= \tan{x}\\
 &= \frac{\sin{x}}{\cos{x}}\\
\frac{dy}{dx} &= \frac{\cos{x} \cos{x} - \sin{x} (-\sin{x}) }{\cos^2{x}} \\
 &= \frac{\cos{x} \cos{x} + \sin{x} \sin{x} }{\cos^2{x}} \\
 &= \frac{ 1 }{\cos^2{x}} \\
 &= \sec^2{x}
\end{align*}
$$

# **Practice:** Trigonometric Derivatives

Let $f(x)=\frac{2 \tan{4x} }{2 - \sec^2{4x}}$.  Calculate $f'(90°)$ and $f''(90°)$. Round your answer to the nearest integer.

<Practice id="trigonometric-derivative-1" answer="8" placeholder="f'(90°)" />
<Practice id="trigonometric-derivative-2" answer="0" placeholder="f''(90°)" />

<Explanation>
**First Derivative:**  
Recall these trigonometric identities.

$$
\begin{align*}
\tan{2x}&=\frac{2\tan{x}}{1-\tan^2{x}}\\
\tan^2{x}+1&=\sec^2{x}
\\
f(x)&=\frac{2\tan{(4x)}}{2-\sec^2{(4x)}}\\
&=\frac{2\tan{(4x)}}{2-(1+\tan^2{(4x)})}\\
&=\frac{2\tan{(4x)}}{1-\tan^2{(4x)})}\\
&=\tan{(8x)}\\
f'(x)&=8\sec^2{(8x)}\\
f'\left ( 90° \right )&= 8\\
\end{align*}
$$

<br />
**Second Derivative:**  

Expand $\sec$ to use the product rule.
$$
\begin{align*}
u=v&=\sec{(8x)}\\
u'=v'&=8\sec{(8x)}\tan{(8x)}\\
\\
f'(x)&=8\sec^2{(8x)}\\
&=8uv\\
f''(x)&=8(u'v+uv')\\
&=8(16\sec^2{(8x)}\tan{(8x)})\\
f''\left ( 90° \right )&=0
\end{align*}
$$
</Explanation>
