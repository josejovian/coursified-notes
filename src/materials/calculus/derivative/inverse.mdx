# Derivatives of Inverse Trigonometric Functions

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*} 
\text{1. }&f(x)=\sin^{-1}(u) \\ 
&f'(x)=\frac{u'}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}\\ 
\text{2. }&f(x)=\cos^{-1}(u)\\ 
&f'(x)=-\frac{u'}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}\\ 
\text{3. }&f(x)=\tan^{-1}(u) \\
 &f'(x)=\frac{u'}{1+u^2}\\ 
\text{4. }&f(x)=\csc^{-1}(u)\\ 
&f'(x)=-\frac{u'}{|u| \sqrt{u^2-1}}\\ 
\text{5. }&f(x)=\sec^{-1}(u)\\
&f'(x)=\frac{u'}{|u| \sqrt{u^2-1}}\\ 
\text{6. }&f(x)=\cot^{-1}(u)\\ 
&f'(x)=\frac{-u'}{1+u^2}\\ 
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

The above derivations can be obtained by using implicit differentiation.

# Derivatives of Inverse Trigonometric Functions: Proof Example

<Example>
Show that $\frac{d}{dx}\sin^{-1}{u} = \frac{u\'}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}$, where $u$ is a function.
</Example>

Recall the trigonometric identity $\sin^2{x} + \cos^2{x} = 1$.

$$
\begin{align*} 
y &= \sin^{-1}{u} \\
\sin{y} &= u \\
\frac{d}{dx} \sin{y} &= \frac{d}{dx} u \\
\frac{dy}{dx} \cos{y} &= \frac{du}{dx} \\
\frac{dy}{dx}  &= \frac{\frac{du}{dx}}{\cos{y}} \\
 &= \frac{\frac{du}{dx}}{\sqrt{1 - \sin^2{y}}} \\
 &= \frac{u'}{\sqrt{1 - u^2}} \\
\end{align*}
$$

# Derivatives of Inverse Trigonometric Functions: Example

<Example>
If $f(x)=\tan^{-1}\left ( \sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}} \right )$, calculate $f'(x)$.
</Example>

Let's focus on the square root's contents first. Recall the chain rule.

$$
\begin{align*} 
u&=\sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}}\\
u'&=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}}} \times \left ( \frac{(x+1) - (x-1)}{(x+1)^2} \right )\\
&=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}}} \times \left ( \frac{2}{(x+1)^2} \right )\\
&=\frac{1}{(x+1)^2 \sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}}}\\
\end{align*}
$$

Continue to the main derivative:

$$
\begin{align*} 
f(x)&=\tan^{-1}\left ( \sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}} \right )\\
f'(x)&=\frac{u'}{u^2+1}\\
&=\frac{\frac{1}{(x+1)^2 \sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}}}}{\frac{x-1}{x+1}+1}\\
&=\frac{\frac{1}{(x+1)^2 \sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}}}}{\frac{2x}{x+1}}\\
&=\frac{1}{2x(x+1)\sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+1}}}\\
\end{align*}
$$
