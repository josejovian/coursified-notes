# Derivatives of Trigonometric Functions

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
1.\quad&f(x)=\sin{x}\\
&f'(x)=\cos{x}\\
2.\quad&f(x)=\cos{x}\\
&f'(x)=-\sin{x}\\
3.\quad&f(x)=\tan{x}\\
&f'(x)=\sec^2{x}\\
4.\quad&f(x)=\csc{x}\\
&f'(x)=-\csc{x}\cot{x}\\
5.\quad&f(x)=\sec{x}\\
&f'(x)=\sec{x}\tan{x}\\
6.\quad&f(x)=\cot{x}\\
&f'(x)=\csc^2{x}\\
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Memorizing the above basic derivation is useful. By using the first two derivation (sin and cos), you can obtain the derivation of the remaining trigonometric functions.

===

# Derivatives of Trigonometric Functions: Examples

<Example>
If $f(x) = 4 \sin^2{2x} - 3 \tan{x}$, calculate $f'(x)$.
</Example>

Remember the chain rule, and the product rule.

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= 4 \sin^2{2x} - 3 \tan{x}\\
&= 4 (\sin{2x} \sin{2x}) - 3 \tan{x}\\
f'(x) &= 4 (2 \cos{2x} \sin{2x} + 2 \sin{2x} \cos{2x}) - 3 \sec^2{x}\\
&= 16 \sin{2x} \cos{2x} - 3 \sec^2{x}\\
\end{align*}
$$

---

<Example>
Show that $\frac{d}{dx}\tan{x} = \sec^2{x}$.
</Example>

Recall the quotient rule, and the trigonometric identity $\sin^2{x}+\cos^2{x} = 1$.

$$
\begin{align*}
y &= \tan{x}\\
 &= \frac{\sin{x}}{\cos{x}}\\
\frac{dy}{dx} &= \frac{\cos{x} \cos{x} - \sin{x} (-\sin{x}) }{\cos^2{x}} \\
 &= \frac{\cos{x} \cos{x} + \sin{x} \sin{x} }{\cos^2{x}} \\
 &= \frac{ 1 }{\cos^2{x}} \\
 &= \sec^2{x}
\end{align*}
$$

# Derivatives of Logarithmic and Exponential Functions

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=\log_a{u}\\
f'(x)&=\frac{u'}{u \ln{a}}
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Provided $u$ is a function, and $a$ is a constant ($a \neq 1$).

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=u^v\\
f'(x)&=u^v (v \ln{u})'
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Provided both $u$ and $v$ are functions, and $a$ is a constant.

===

# Derivatives of Logarithmic and Exponential Functions: Example

<Example>
If $f(x) = - \ln{\left | \cos{2x} \right |}$, calculate $f'(x)$.
</Example>

Remember the chain rule. Consider $p(x) = \cos{2x}$ and $q(x) = \ln{\left | x \right |}$. Then,  
$$
\begin{align*}
p(x)&=\cos{2x}\\
p'(x)&=-2\sin{2x}\\
q(x)&=\ln{\left | x \right |}\\
q'(x)&=\frac{1}{x}\\
\end{align*}
$$

Do the main derivation:
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=- \ln{\left | \cos{2x} \right |}\\
&=- q(p(x)) \\
f'(x)&=- q'(p(x)) . p'(x) \\
&=- \frac{1}{\cos{2x}} \times -2\sin{2x} \\
&=- \frac{-2\sin{2x}}{\cos{2x}}\\
&=2 \tan{2x}
\end{align*}
$$
