# Derivatives of Logarithmic and Exponential Functions

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=\log_a{u}\\
f'(x)&=\frac{u'}{u \ln{a}}
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Provided $u$ is a function, and $a$ is a constant ($a \neq 1$).

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=u^v\\
f'(x)&=u^v (v \ln{u})'
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Provided both $u$ and $v$ are functions, and $a$ is a constant.

# Derivatives of Logarithmic and Exponential Functions: Example

<Example>
If $f(x) = - \ln{\left | \cos{2x} \right |}$, calculate $f'(x)$.
</Example>

Remember the chain rule. Consider $p(x) = \cos{2x}$ and $q(x) = \ln{\left | x \right |}$. Then,  
$$
\begin{align*}
p(x)&=\cos{2x}\\
p'(x)&=-2\sin{2x}\\
q(x)&=\ln{\left | x \right |}\\
q'(x)&=\frac{1}{x}\\
\end{align*}
$$

Do the main derivation:
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=- \ln{\left | \cos{2x} \right |}\\
&=- q(p(x)) \\
f'(x)&=- q'(p(x)) . p'(x) \\
&=- \frac{1}{\cos{2x}} \times -2\sin{2x} \\
&=- \frac{-2\sin{2x}}{\cos{2x}}\\
&=2 \tan{2x}
\end{align*}
$$

# **Practice:** Logarithmic and Exponential Derivatives

Let $f(x)=e^{x^2}\ln{\left|x\right|}$.  Calculate $f'(2)$. Express your answer using the following approximation: $e^4=54.6$ and $\ln(2)=0.69$.

<Practice id="logarithmic-derivative-1" answer="177.996" />

<Explanation>
Use the product rule.
$$
\begin{align*}
u&=e^{x^2}\\
u'&=2x e^{x^2}\\
v&=\ln{|x|}\\
v'&=\frac{1}{x}\\
\\
f(x)&=e^{x^2}\ln{\left|x\right|}\\
&=uv\\
f'(x)&=u'v+uv'\\
&=2x e^{x^2} \ln{|x|} + \frac{e^{x^2}}{x}\\
f'(2)&=2(2) e^{2^2} \ln{|2|} + \frac{e^{2^2}}{2}\\
&=4 e^{4} \ln{|2|} + \frac{e^{4}}{2}\\
&=4 (54.6) (0.69) + \frac{54.6}{2}\\
&=177.996\\
\end{align*}
$$
</Explanation>
