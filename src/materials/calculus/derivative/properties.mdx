# Properties of Derivatives (1)

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=c\\
f'(x)&=0
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Derivative of a constant function is zero.

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=cu\\
f'(x)&=cu'
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Provided $u$ is a function, and $c$ is a constant.

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=x^n\\
f'(x)&=nx^{n-1}
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

The power rule is as above, provided $n$ is a real number.

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=u \pm v\\
f'(x)&=u' \pm v'
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Provided $u$ and $v$ are functions.

# Properties of Derivatives (1): Exampless

<Example>If $f(x) = (x+2)^2$, calculate $f'(x)$.</Example>

Expand first, then calculate:

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= (x+2)^2\\
&= x^2+4x+4\\
f'(x) &= 2x+4\\
\end{align*}
$$

---

<Example>
If $f(x) = \frac{2x^3 - 5x^2 - 3x + 1}{x^3}$, calculate $f'(x)$.
</Example>

Simplify first, then calculate.

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= \frac{2x^3 - 5x^2 - 3x + 1}{5x^2}\\
&= \frac{2}{5}x - 1 - \frac{3}{5x} + \frac{1}{5x^2}\\
f'(x) &= \frac{2}{5} + \frac{3}{5x^2} - \frac{2}{5x^3}\\
\end{align*}
$$

# Properties of Derivatives (2)

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=u.v\\
f'(x)&=u'v + uv'
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

The product rule is as above, provided $u$ and $v$ are functions.

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=\frac{u}{v}\\
f'(x)&=\frac{u'v + uv'}{v^2}
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

The quotient rule is as above, provided $u$ and $v$ are functions.

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=g(h(x))\\
f'(x)&=g'(h(x)).h'(x)
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

The chain rule is as above, provided $g$ and $h$ are functions, where $h$ is differentiable at $x$, and $g$ is differentiable at $h(x)$.

# Properties of Derivatives (2): Example

<Example>If $f(x) = 2x^2 (4x+1)^5$, calculate $f'(x)$.</Example>

Let $u = 2x^2$ and $v = (4x+1)^5$. Deriving these individually yields:

$$
\begin{align*}
u &= 2x^2\\
u' &= 4x\\
v &= (4x+1)^5\\
v' &= 20(4x+1)^4
\end{align*}
$$

Then, calculate the main derivative:

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= uv\\
&= u'v + uv'\\
&= 4x(4x+1)^5 + 40x^2(4x+1)^4
\end{align*}
$$

# **Practice**: Derivative

Let $f$ and $g$ be a function, such that $f(x) = \frac{\sqrt{x + 2}}{g(x)}$. The definition of $g(x)$ is unknown, but we know that $g(2) = 1$ and $g'(2) = 1$. Calculate $f'(2)$.

<Practice id="derivative-1" answer="-1.75" />

<Explanation>
Use the product rule, where $u = \sqrt{x + 2}$ and $v = g(x)$:
$$
\begin{align*}
u &= \sqrt{x + 2}\\
u' &= \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x + 2}}\\
v &= g(x)\\
v' &= g'(x)
\end{align*}
$$

Then, calculate the main derivative. We don't necessarily have to know $g(x)$ to do this.

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= \frac{u}{v}\\
f'(x)&= \frac{u'v + uv'}{v^2}\\
&= \frac{\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x + 2}}g(x) + \sqrt{x + 2} g'(x)}{(g'(x))^2}\\
\end{align*}
$$

Then, substitute all the known values:

$$
\begin{align*}
f'(x) &= \frac{\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x + 2}}g(x) - \sqrt{x + 2} g'(x)}{(g'(x))^2}\\
&= \frac{\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x + 2}}g(x) - \sqrt{x + 2} g'(x)}{(g'(x))^2}\\
&= \frac{\frac{1}{2\sqrt{(2) + 2}}(1) - \sqrt{(2) + 2} (1)}{1}\\
&= \frac{\frac{1}{4} - 2}{1}\\
&= -1.75\\
\end{align*}
$$

</Explanation>

# **Practice**: Derivative

Let $f(x) = \left(4x+c\right)^{2}(x-2)$, where $c$ is an unknown constant no more than $17$. It is known that $f'\left (-\frac{1}{2} \right) = -64$. Determine the value of $c$.

<Practice id="derivative-2" answer="6" />

<Explanation>
Let $u = \left(4x+c\right)^{2}$ and $v = x-2$:
$$
\begin{align*}
u &= \left(4x+c\right)^{2}\\
u' &= 2(4)(4x+c)\\
v &= x - 2\\
v' &= 1
\end{align*}
$$

Then, calculate the main derivative. We don't necessarily have to know $c$ to do this.

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= uv\\
f'(x)&= u'v + uv'\\
&= 2(4)(4x+c)(x-2) + \left(4x+c\right)^{2}\\
&= 8(4x^2 - 8x + cx -2c) + (16x^2 + 8cx + c^2)\\
\end{align*}
$$

Then, substitute all the known values to get $c$.

$$
\begin{align*}
f'(x)&= 8(4x^2 - 8x + cx -2c) + (16x^2 + 8cx + c^2)\\
f'\left (-\frac{1}{2} \right) &= 8(1 + 4 - \frac{c}{2} - 2c) + (4 - 4c + c^2)\\
-64 &= 8(1 + 4 - \frac{c}{2} - 2c) + (4 - 4c + c^2)\\
-64 &= (40 - 20c) + (4 - 4c + c^2)\\
0 &= c^2 - 24c + 108\\
0 &= (c-6)(c-18)\\
\end{align*}
$$

It appears there are two possible values for $c$. However, the question already declared that $c$ is less than $17$. In this case, the appropriate value for c is $6$.
</Explanation>

# **Practice**: Derivative

A certain factory manufactures a specific type of product for $x$ days for the daily cost of $(4x + \frac{100}{x} + 40)$ million IDR. Calculate the cheapest daily cost possible. Answer in terms of million IDR.

<Practice id="derivative-3" answer="80" />

<Explanation>
When a function reaches its maximum or minimum value, the rate of change at that point is zero. In other words, $f'(x)=0$.

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= 4x + \frac{100}{x} + 40\\
f'(x)&= 4 - \frac{100}{x^2}\\
0 &= 4 - \frac{100}{x^2}\\
4 &= \frac{100}{x^2}\\
x &= 5\\
\end{align*}
$$

The daily cost will be the cheapest when $x=5$. Substitute this to the initial function to obtain the cheapest cost possible.

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= 4x + \frac{100}{x} + 40\\
f(5) &= 20 + 20 + 40\\
f(5) &= 80\\
\end{align*}
$$

</Explanation>
