# Properties of Derivatives (1)

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=c\\
f'(x)&=0
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Derivative of a constant function is zero.

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=cu\\
f'(x)&=cu'
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Provided $u$ is a function, and $c$ is a constant.

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=x^n\\
f'(x)&=nx^{n-1}
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

The power rule is as above, provided $n$ is a real number.

---

<Formula>
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=u \pm v\\
f'(x)&=u' \pm v'
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

Provided $u$ and $v$ are functions.

# Properties of Derivatives (1): Exampless

<Example>
Calculate the first derivative of the following:
1. $f(x) = (x+2)^2$
2. $f(x) = \frac{2x^3 - 5x^2 - 3x + 1}{x^3}$
</Example>

**Number 1**  
Expand first, then calculate:

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= (x+2)^2\\
&= x^2+4x+4\\
f'(x) &= 2x+4\\
\end{align*}
$$

**Number 2**  
Simplify first, then calculate.

$$
\begin{align*}
f(x) &= \frac{2x^3 - 5x^2 - 3x + 1}{5x^2}\\
&= \frac{2}{5}x - 1 - \frac{3}{5x} + \frac{1}{5x^2}\\
f'(x) &= \frac{2}{5} + \frac{3}{5x^2} - \frac{2}{5x^3}\\
\end{align*}
$$

# Properties of Derivatives (2)

<Formula>
**Product Rule***
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=u.v\\
f'(x)&=u'v + uv'
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

---

<Formula>
**Quotient Rule**
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=\frac{u}{v}\\
f'(x)&=\frac{u'v + uv'}{v^2}
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

---

<Formula>
**Chain Rule**
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)&=g(h(x))\\
f'(x)&=g'(h(x)).h'(x)
\end{align*}
$$
</Formula>

# Properties of Derivatives (2): Example

<Example>
Calculate the first derivative of the following:
1. $p(x) = \frac{(4x^2+4x+1)}{(x^2+4x+4)}$
2. $q(x) = 2x^2 (4x+1)^5$
</Example>

**Number 1**  
Use the quotient rule. Let $u = 4x^2+4x+1$ and $v = x^2+4x+4$:
$$
\begin{align*}
u &= 4x^2+4x+1\\
u' &= 8x + 4\\
v &= x^2+4x+4\\
v' &=2x + 4
\end{align*}
$$

Then, calculate the main derivative:
$$
\begin{align*}
p(x) &= uv\\
p'(x)&= \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}\\
&= \frac{(8x + 4)(x^2 + 4x + 4) - (4x^2 + 4x + 1)(2x + 4)}{(x^2+4x+4)^2}\\
&= \frac{(8x + 4)(x+2)^2 - (4x^2 + 4x + 1)(2)(x + 2)}{(x+2)^4}\\
&= \frac{(8x + 4)(x+2) - (4x^2 + 4x + 1)(2)}{(x+2)^3}\\
&= \frac{(8x ^2 + 20x + 8) - (8x^2 + 8x + 2)}{(x+2)^3}\\
&= \frac{12x + 6}{(x+2)^3}
\end{align*}
$$

**Number 2**  
Product rule is used here, as well as chain rule. 
For the product rule, let $u = 2x^2$ and $v = (4x+1)^5$.
For the chain rule, let $g(x)=x^5$ and $h(x)=4x+1$, which when combined makes $v=g(h(x))=(4x+1)^5$.
$$
\begin{align*}
g(x) &= x^5\\
g'(x) &= 5x^4\\
h(x) &= (4x+1)\\
h'(x) &= 4\\
\\
v&= g(h(x))\\
v'&= h'(x) . g'(h(x))\\
&= 20 (4x + 1)^4\\
\end{align*}
$$

Return to the product rule.  
$$
\begin{align*}
u &= 2x^2\\
u' &= 4x\\
v &= (4x+1)^5\\
v' &= 20(4x+1)^4
\end{align*}
$$

Then, calculate the main derivative:

$$
\begin{align*}
q(x) &= uv\\
q'(x)&= u'v + uv'\\
&= 4x(4x+1)^5 + 40x^2(4x+1)^4
\end{align*}
$$
